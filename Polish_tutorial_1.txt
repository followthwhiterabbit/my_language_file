THE ACCUSATIVE 

If we are going to use one of the following verbs in a sentence. 

lubic - to like 
miec  - to have
jesc - to eat 
pic - to drink 
kochac - to love 
										

																					masculine       |       feminine        |         neuter 
																				
																				animate   | inanimate           -a					no change 
						
												adjective(e.g big	|			-ego        no change     	
												or pronoun(e.g my)  |
																	|
													noun			|            -a         no change           -e                  no change  
											(e.g a dog, a coffee)   |
													


											As you can see in the table above, we can divide masculine nouns into two groups: 
												animate and personal (essentially people, animals, fruit, vegetables, names of cars), 
												inanimate (other nouns)

											Lubie kawe. - (I like coffee. )

NOMINATIVE 

In the nominative (a basic form), we use the following question words: kto? - who? , co? - what? 
									but in the accusative questions we have : kogo ? - who ? co ? - what? 
						
Kto jest ? - Who is it ? (nominative ? ) 
Kogo kochasz / lubisz ? - Who do you love/like (accusative) ? 

We never change the endings of the adverbs (e.g najbardziej). We only change the endings of the adjectives, nouns and pronouns. 



If the last consonant in the masucline adjective is k or g, you should keep the i before the accusative ending, e.g niski - niskiego
																													drogi - drogiego

There are three words you can choose from to express or: 

	OR ----> lub / albo / czy 

There is no difference between lub && albo , both are used in affirmative or negative sentences 


What is important to remember is that czy is used only in questions ? 

Rano pije kawe lub/albo herbate. - I drink coffee or tea in the morning. 

Chcesz kawe czy herbate ? - Do you want coffee or tea ?
